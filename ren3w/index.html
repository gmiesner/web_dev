What is systemic racism?

Systemic racism is racism that is embeded into our society's laws and policies, based on a history of slavery and discrimination. Because of this, people of color continue to face inequalities in multiple aspects of life, including housing, education, employment, and more.

How does systemic racism relate to environmental racism?

Systemic racism is compounded with environmental issues, such as lead poisoning and mold, to create environmental racism that disproportionately effects predominantly POC neighborhoods, especially black communities. These issues cause higher poverty rates, lower quality of life, and many health issues in communities.

About Renew

Renew was created to offer a more accessable way to learn about environmental racism in St. Louis. While there is a lot of information available online, it tends to be scattered across the web, often in inaccessible ways that make it harder to learn.
